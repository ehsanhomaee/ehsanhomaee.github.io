About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Physics (Biophysics) at the University of Illinois Urbana–Champaign (UIUC), advised by Prof. Ido Golding at the Loomis Laboratory of Physics. I am also completing a concurrent M.Sc. in Statistics at UIUC.

My research sits at the crossroads of quantitative biology, machine learning, and statistical modeling. I develop computational tools to understand how individual viruses make decisions — how a single bacteriophage "chooses" between lysis and lysogeny, and what molecular mechanisms underlie that choice.

I combine spatial transcriptomics (seqFISH), probabilistic inference, and biophysical modeling to reconstruct single-virus transcriptomes and quantify transcriptional heterogeneity at unprecedented resolution. I also develop genome-wide models linking transcriptional regulation to DNA replication in bacteria.

Beyond the lab, I am passionate about the outdoors, community service, and endurance challenges. I am a member of I4K (Illini 4000), where I fundraise to support children affected by cancer — including an upcoming cross-country biking journey planned for summer 2026.
